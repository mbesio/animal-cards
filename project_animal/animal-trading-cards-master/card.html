Bear

There are three species of bears in North America. The most common is the American black bear (Ursus americanus). Unfortunately named, the black bear can be various shades of black, white and brown.

  • Scientific Name: Ursidae
  • Lifespan: 20 years
  • Weigth: 270 kg
  • Location: A lot in Canada

The Bear is a well-known animal. It is a big animal with either lighter or darker brown fur. Tail is short and not well seen among its fur. The young have a white collar around their neck, sometimes the adults also still have it. Bears walk like humans do on their soles. It has dull teeth as all these who eat both plants and animals.
